\section{The formation of cores}
In a molecular cloud, a local density enhancement can trigger gravitational collapse, forming a prestellar core. As the collapse proceeds, the prestellar core becomes a protostellar core.
During the early class 0 or class I stage, these systems are still deeply embedded within their envelope. Within this turbulent environment, small fluctuations can lead to gravitational instability, giving rise to an asymmetric, filamentary structure known as the streamer.
Streamer is the density enhancement structures within the protostellar envelope. The key characteristics are that do not necessarily follow the Keplerian rotation. They have been considered as a channel to carry mass onto the disk.

\section{The streamer in observations}
Recently, several observations revealed asymmetric structures in the protostellar system (e.g., \citealt{Pineda_2020}, \citealt{Valdivia-Mena_2022}, \citealt{Hsieh_2023}). The features of streamers are very diverse in morphology, do not always form in a disk plane, and have very weak density enhancement in observation. 

\section{Observations}
To test our model against sources where streamers have been detected, we use five observational datasets: Per-emb-2, Per-emb-50, S CrA, HL Tau, and SVS 13A. The Per-emb-2, Per-emb-50, and SVS 13A were observed by the Northern Extended Millimeter Array (NOEMA). Per-emb-2 was observed in D-configuration on June 22, July 27-28, and August 19, 2018, and in C-configuration on October 9, 10, 17, and 18 (project S18AG, \citealt{Pineda_2020}). The baseline values ranged from 16.4 to 287.8 meters. Per-emb-50 was observed in C-configuration on December 29, 2019, and January 5, 2021, and D-configuration on August 6, September 7, and 8, 2020 (\citealt{Valdivia-Mena_2022}). SVS13A was also observed using two different configurations: C-configuration on December 29, 2019, and January 5, 2020, and in D-configuration on August 6 and September 7, 2020. The baseline range is between 15.6 and 368 meters, corresponding to a synthesized beam size of $1.^"2 \times 0.^"7$ (\citealt{Hsieh_2023}). The S CrA , HL Tau were observed by Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA). The S CrA observation data from ALMA Science Archrive was observed on November 6, 2019 (Project 2019.1.01792.S, \citealt{Gupta_2023}). HL Tau was observed by ALMA's C40-7 array configuration on August 22, 28, and September 1, 2017. The maximum projected baseline length is ~3.3 km (\citealt{Yen_2019}). For each source, we extract the position-position-velocity (PPV) data of the streamer.
