Streamers have been observed with high-resolution ALMA observations around many protoplanetary disks undergoing formation. They have been suggested to dominate the mass accretion budget from the collapsing prestellar core and to have important consequences for disk dynamics. Understanding the formation of streamers is therefore essential for explaining how protoplanetary disks receive mass from their surrounding envelopes. We propose a model to explain the formation of streamer structures by considering density enhancements arising from gravitational instability. We test our model against sources where streamers have been detected, including Per-emb-2 and Per-emb-50 observed with NOEMA, as well as SCrA and HLTau observed with ALMA, and fit for the model parameters. This approach allows us to gain deeper insights into the physical origin of streamers and their role in mass transport from the core to the disk. The model can be applied to analyze both new observations and archival data that show signatures of streamers.
